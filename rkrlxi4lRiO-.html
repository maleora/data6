More About The Hundredth Year of the War

This is very powerful despite its brevity. It's a tribute to all the Marlenes of the world, who stay curious, ask tough questions, and keep trying to do the right thing whether they're successful or not. This is a very effective anti-war piece, even if it's ultra-compressed (a mere 12 pages on an e-reader). I started going all squishy about half-way through, and kept on for half an hour afterwards; and that's a sign I thought the writing was excellent and was being emotionally buffeted by it.
